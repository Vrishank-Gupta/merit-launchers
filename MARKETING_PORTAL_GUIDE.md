# Merit Launchers — Marketing Partner Portal
## Business Guide: How It Works for Every User

---

## What Is the Marketing Partner Portal?

The marketing partner portal is a dedicated web platform that lets Merit Launchers run, track, and grow its partner (associate) network. It has two separate sides:

- **Marketing Admin Portal** — used internally by the Merit Launchers marketing team to manage partners, commissions, payouts, and marketing materials.
- **Partner Portal** — used by associates (Campus Ambassadors, Education Associates, Institutional Partners) to track their referrals, earnings, and grow their own network.

Both are accessible from the main Merit Launchers website. No separate app download is needed.

---

## The Four People in This System

| Who | What They Do |
|---|---|
| **Marketing Admin** | Runs the entire partner program. Creates partners, sets commissions, approves applications, processes payouts. |
| **Partner / Associate** | Refers students and onboards new partners. Earns commission on sales. Tracks performance via their own dashboard. |
| **Prospective Partner** | Someone a partner wants to bring into the network. Fills a self-registration form. Gets activated by the Marketing Admin. |
| **Student** | Clicks a partner's referral link. Gets attributed to that partner when they sign up or purchase. |

# PERSONA 1: Marketing Admin

**Who uses this:** The internal Merit Launchers team member managing the partner program.

**Where they log in:** `meritlaunchers.com/marketing-admin/login`

---

## What the Marketing Admin Can Do

### 1. See the Overview Dashboard

The first thing the Marketing Admin sees after logging in is a summary of the entire partner program:

- **Total active partners** in the network
- **Total revenue** generated across all partners ever
- **Total pending payout amount** owed to partners across all months
- **Alert banner** if there are new partner applications waiting to be reviewed — with a direct link to review them

This gives an instant health check of the program without needing to dig into individual records.

---

### 2. Manage Partners

**Partners List page** shows every partner in the system with:
- Their name, referral code, partner type
- Total students they've referred
- Total revenue they've generated
- Total paid enrollments

From here the admin can:
- **View a partner's full profile and performance**
- **Create a new partner directly** (when the admin is onboarding someone themselves)
- **Edit a partner's details**

---

#### Creating a Partner Directly

The admin fills a form with:
- **Name and Associate ID** — e.g. "Manish Sharma" / "ML-A102"
- **Partner Type** — Campus Ambassador, Education Associate, or Institutional Partner
- **Referral Code** — a unique code like `MANISHML` that students use when signing up; this is how all referrals are tracked
- **Login Email and Password** — the credentials the partner will use to log into the partner portal
- **Starting Commission Slab (%)** — the percentage of revenue this partner earns, e.g. 10%
- **Bank / UPI Details** — optional, used when processing payouts

Once created, the partner can immediately log in. The admin shares their credentials directly (by WhatsApp, email, etc.).

---

#### Viewing a Partner's Full Profile

Clicking on any partner opens their detailed profile, which shows:

**Profile Information**
- Name, code, associate ID, partner type, date joined

**Commission Slab History**
- A full log of every commission rate this partner has ever had, with the dates each rate was active
- Example: Started at 10% in January, moved to 15% in March

**All Their Referred Students**
- A table of every student who signed up using this partner's referral code
- Shows: student name, city, when they joined, how many courses they've purchased, and total amount spent

**Click Analytics**
- How many unique clicks each of their referral links has received, broken down by channel (WhatsApp, Instagram, Direct, YouTube, Seminar, etc.)

**Payout History**
- Every monthly payout for this partner — the month, revenue, commission earned, status (pending or paid), and payment details

---

#### Updating a Partner's Commission Rate

At any time, the admin can assign a new commission slab to a partner. When this is done:
- The old rate is automatically closed off with an end date
- The new rate starts from the date the admin specifies
- The full history is preserved — this matters because monthly payouts are calculated using a weighted average if the rate changed mid-month

**Example:** If a partner was at 10% for the first 15 days of a month and 15% for the remaining 16 days, their commission for that month is calculated proportionally — not simply at the new rate.

---

### 3. Review and Approve Partner Applications

When an existing partner shares their onboarding link with someone and that person fills in the registration form, a **pending application** lands here.

Each application card shows:
- The applicant's name, phone, partner type they selected
- The auto-generated referral code
- Which partner referred them
- When they applied

To approve, the admin fills in:
- **Login Email** — what the new partner will use to sign in
- **Password** — their initial password
- **Associate ID** — the internal ID (e.g. ML-A103)
- **Starting Commission Slab** — their initial earnings rate

Clicking **Approve & Activate** instantly activates the account. The new partner can log in immediately. The admin then shares these credentials with them.

If an application is not right, the admin can simply leave it pending or delete it — the applicant cannot log in until approved.

---

### 4. Generate and Process Monthly Payouts

**Payouts page** is where the admin handles commission payments to all partners.

**Step 1 — Generate payouts for a month**

The admin selects a month (e.g. March 2026) and clicks Generate. The system automatically:
- Looks at every purchase made by students referred by each partner during that month
- Calculates each partner's revenue figure
- Applies the correct commission rate (weighted average if the rate changed mid-month)
- Creates a payout record for each partner who had sales that month

**Step 2 — Mark as Paid**

Once the admin actually transfers the money (bank transfer, UPI, etc.), they come back and mark each payout as Paid:
- Enter the actual amount paid
- Add a reference note (e.g. UTR number, UPI reference)
- The record is timestamped and locked

Partners can see this on their own dashboard — they know exactly what they've been paid and what's pending.

---

### 5. Manage the Marketing Toolkit

The **Toolkit** is a file library that partners can download from their own portal.

The admin can:
- **Upload files** — posters, social media creatives, WhatsApp message templates, webinar invite templates, referral link guides, etc.
- **Categorise each file** — e.g. "Poster", "Template", "Social", "Guide"
- **Delete files** they no longer want available

Partners see and download these files from their own Toolkit page. The admin controls what's available.

# PERSONA 2: Partner / Associate

**Who uses this:** Campus Ambassadors, Education Associates, and Institutional Partners who bring students and new partners into the Merit Launchers ecosystem.

**Where they log in:** `meritlaunchers.com/partner/login`
Credentials are given to them by the Marketing Admin.

---

## What a Partner Sees First: The Dashboard

The dashboard is a real-time snapshot of everything the partner has achieved. It shows:

**Five key numbers at the top:**
- **Students Referred** — total students who signed up using their code, with a split of how many are paid vs. free
- **Revenue Generated** — total money those students have spent on courses
- **Commission Earned** — total commission they've been paid so far
- **Pending Commission** — commission that's been calculated but not yet paid out
- **Conversion Rate** — percentage of their referred students who actually bought a course

**Smart Alerts** — personalised nudges that appear automatically based on the partner's situation:
- A reminder if they have pending commission waiting
- A warning if their conversion rate is low, with a suggestion to improve
- A celebration if they've hit a milestone (e.g. 50+ students referred)
- A prompt to start sharing their link if no clicks have been tracked yet

**Conversion Funnel Chart** — a visual bar chart showing:
Referral Link Clicks → Free Signups → Paid Students
This helps partners understand where they're losing people in the funnel.

**Channel Clicks Breakdown** — shows how many clicks came from each source (WhatsApp, Instagram, Direct, YouTube, Seminar, etc.) and what proportion of total clicks each channel represents.

---

## Referral Links — How Partners Share

Every partner has two links visible in their sidebar and on the Network page:

### Student Referral Link
`meritlaunchers.com/ref/PARTNERCODE`

Share this with students. When someone clicks it:
- They are redirected to the Play Store (to download the app) or the Merit Launchers website
- A tracking tag is saved in their browser for 30 days
- When they sign up during those 30 days, they are automatically attributed to this partner
- The click itself is recorded for analytics

Partners can also share **channel-specific versions** of this link:
- `/ref/CODE/wa` for WhatsApp
- `/ref/CODE/ig` for Instagram
- `/ref/CODE/yt` for YouTube
- `/ref/CODE/seminar` for seminar audiences

This lets them know which platform is actually driving traffic.

### Partner Onboarding Link
`meritlaunchers.com/join/PARTNERCODE`

Share this with someone they want to bring in as a fellow partner. When someone opens it, they see a registration form. After they fill it, their application goes to the Marketing Admin for approval.

### Viewing a Sub-Partner's Performance

When a partner clicks View on one of their sub-partners, they see a detailed breakdown:

- Payout history (month-by-month)
- Recent students list
- Commission slab history

This gives full visibility into how the people they brought in are performing — which is useful for coaching, motivating, and holding their network accountable.

The partner **cannot see the sub-partner's login email, contact details, or bank information** — only performance data.

# PERSONA 3: Prospective Partner

**Who this is:** Someone who has been approached by an existing partner and wants to join the Merit Launchers associate program.

**Where they go:** `meritlaunchers.com/join/PARTNERCODE`

This link is shared by the existing partner (from their sidebar or Network page).

---

## The Registration Flow

When the prospective partner opens the link, they see a clean registration page that shows who referred them (the referral code is visible).

They fill in:
- **Full Name** (required)
- **Phone Number** (required)
- **Email Address**
- **City**
- **Partner Type** — Campus Ambassador, Education Associate, or Institutional Partner

They click **Submit Application**.

A confirmation screen appears: *"Application submitted! You will receive login credentials once approved by the team."*

That's it. The ball is now in the Marketing Admin's court.

**What happens next:**
- The application appears in the Marketing Admin's Pending queue
- The admin reviews it, sets their login credentials and commission rate, and approves it
- The applicant is then contacted with their login details (by the admin or the referring partner, out-of-band)
- They log in at `meritlaunchers.com/partner/login`

**If they try to log in before approval:** They see a clear message — *"Account pending approval. You'll receive login credentials once approved."*

# PERSONA 4: Student

**Who this is:** A student who clicked a partner's referral link. They don't interact with the partner portal at all — but their actions drive everything.

---

## What Happens When a Student Clicks a Referral Link

**Step 1 — They click the link**
The partner shared something like `meritlaunchers.com/ref/MANISHML/wa`

**Step 2 — Redirect**
The student is immediately redirected to:
- The Google Play Store (to download the app), if the app link is configured
- Or the Merit Launchers website home page

A small tracking tag is invisibly saved in their browser. It remembers which partner's code they came from and through which channel (WhatsApp in this case). This tag lasts for **30 days**.

**Step 3 — They sign up**
When the student downloads the app or visits the website and creates an account within 30 days of clicking the link, their account is linked to that partner's referral code.

**Step 4 — Attribution is permanent**
Once linked, all their activity is counted against that partner forever:
- Their signup appears in the partner's student list
- Any courses they purchase count toward the partner's revenue and commission
- Their exam attempts are counted in the partner's stats

**Step 5 — The partner earns**
When the student buys a course, the partner earns their commission rate (e.g. 10%) on that purchase. At the end of the month, this is compiled into a payout.

# Key Rules to Know

**Referral attribution**
A student is attributed to the partner whose link they clicked most recently, within the last 30 days. If they click two different partners' links, the second one wins.

**Commission is on net sales only**
Only completed purchases count. Free signups generate no commission.

**Commission is calculated monthly**
The admin generates payouts once a month. Partners don't get paid automatically — the admin reviews and manually marks payments as done.

**Commission rate changes are pro-rated**
If a partner's rate changes mid-month, their commission for that month is calculated day-by-day. Each day uses whatever rate was active on that day.

**Sub-partner visibility is limited**
A partner can see their direct sub-partners' performance. They cannot see sub-partners two levels below them. And they can only see identity information (not financial data) about the person who onboarded them.

**Pending partners cannot log in**
A self-registered applicant has no access until the Marketing Admin explicitly approves them. There is no auto-approval.

**Referral codes are unique and permanent**
Once a referral code is assigned to a partner, it cannot be reused by anyone else. All historical data is tied to that code.
